Jane Christie

  • daughter of Yuan and Agatha Christie
    • second oldest of her family; older twin of Mimuka
  • bigender (man & woman) woman, trans
    • she/her
  • abroflux bisexual lesbian
  • starts the series at 12 and ends at 19
  • born December 16th, 2002
  • Namesake: Jane Austen
  • Ability: Pride and Prejudice
    • Ability Details: An augmenting Ability that works to strengthen and refine someone else's Ability, or if they don't have an Ability, their physical strength, but it only works on people she shares mutual respect with (as in, she respects them and they respect her in turn). She can also control how much her Ability will augment theirs, but it is limited to the amount of mutual respect they share; for example, if they both mutually respect each other, but they only respect her a little bit, it will only augment their Ability a very small amount. Thanks to its conditions for usage, she can essentially subtly "test" to see if and how much someone actually respects her by trying to use her Ability on them, as they won't necessarily know she's doing it unless she tells them, since she can control how strong it gets incrementally.
  • Affiliation: she is the heir to the Order of the Clock Tower, seeing how her older brother and the oldest Christie child, Richard Christie, has no interest in being a part of the organization. After she and the other Christie children start attending the International Academy for Gifted Students, she also affiliates with the Gunslinger Kids, and intends to ally the Order of the Clock Tower with them once she takes control of it.
  • Level at which I think about them: low. She's one of the newer fankids I have, and was made back in April
  • Brief Notes:
    • Jane's the type of girl who is driven by ambition and determined to succeed at literally everything she does. Her professional life is more important to her than almost anything else; the only thing she finds more important is her family. Her younger twin sister, Mimuka, is her best and closest friend, but even she isn't always safe from the criticizing comments Jane makes about literally everyone she's ever known. It's important to note that Jane doesn't always mean to criticize people in an unkind way, though; often times with those she's close to and cares about, it's just how she shows her love. The most frequent target of her ire, however, is her own self; no one holds Jane to a higher standard than Jane herself does.
